In modern day world, between 33-50% of all food produced globally is never eaten, and the statistics show that value of this wasted food is worth over $1 trillion. In India this problem has its own face. According to the United Nations Development Programme, up to 40% of the food produced in India is wasted, which means Indians waste as much food as the whole of United Kingdom consumes.

All this mess is mainly due to our lack of ability to manage the leftover food. There are many instances where if we have extra food because of any events or party it is simply dumped. Even if we want to give it to any orphanage or poor people many a times we don’t have time or don’t have an idea about that.

What is Feed The Need?


The project FEED THE NEED, a food redistribution system is an online solution for food-sharing, aims to reduce food wastage. The main problem of existing system is in restaurants, in any functions or in any house if there is any surplus food it ends up with garbage because instantly there is no way to share that food with anyone. So this project include the development of a web application software which helps us to sponsor that extra food to poor people or nearby orphanage. It does this by connecting those with surplus food to those who need or wish to consume such food.

Here, at FEED THE NEED, we believe that our small actions can lead to big changes.





Our Vision

FEED THE NEED is simply to feed people and to reduce the wastage of surplus food.
